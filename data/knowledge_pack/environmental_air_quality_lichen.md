# Environmental Air Quality Lichen Playbook

source_id: local:air_quality_lichen

## purpose
Support citizen-science screening for possible air quality patterns using lichen observations.

## signs
- Sparse, damaged, or changing lichen growth near roads or industrial areas.
- Differences between a suspected impact site and cleaner reference site.
- Dust, smoke, odor, or traffic exposure context.

## immediate_actions
- Record location, date, weather, nearby roads or sources, and lichen abundance from a safe public area.
- Compare observations with a reference area and repeat over time.

## avoid
- Do not infer exact pollutant concentrations or health diagnoses from lichen alone.
- Do not trespass, climb, or sample protected habitats without permission.
- Do not identify private property owners or individuals.

## when_to_call_emergency_services
- Call local emergency services for acute smoke, toxic odor, chemical release, breathing distress, or immediate public danger.

## calm_mode_guidance
- Treat lichen as a screening bioindicator that should be paired with official monitoring or expert review.
- Build repeatable routes and photo points for community comparison.

## responder_packet_fields
- lichen_abundance
- site_type
- distance_from_road
- odor_or_smoke_context
- reference_site_available

## safety_notes
- Lichen patterns can reflect environmental stress but are not a standalone diagnosis.
- Public health claims require official data or expert review.

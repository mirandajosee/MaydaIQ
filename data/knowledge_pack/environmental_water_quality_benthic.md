# Environmental Water Quality Benthic Playbook

source_id: local:water_quality_benthic

## purpose
Support citizen-science screening for possible water quality concerns using safe observations and benthic bioindicator context.

## signs
- Cloudy water, unusual odor or color, foam, algae bloom, dead fish, or low benthic macroinvertebrate diversity.
- Recent stormwater runoff, construction, discharge, or spill reports.

## immediate_actions
- Avoid contact with suspicious water.
- Document location, date, weather, odor, color, visible organisms, and photos from a safe distance.
- Compare upstream and downstream observations when it is safe.

## avoid
- Do not claim an exact contaminant or cause without lab or agency confirmation.
- Do not enter unsafe banks, fast water, private property, or contaminated water.
- Do not collect samples without local protocol and protective equipment.

## when_to_call_emergency_services
- Call local emergency services for chemical spill, strong fumes, mass fish kill, public exposure, or immediate health danger.

## calm_mode_guidance
- Use benthic observations as screening evidence, not diagnosis.
- Repeat observations over time and pair with official sampling where possible.
- Route structured reports to environmental agencies or watershed groups.

## responder_packet_fields
- water_color
- odor
- algae_or_foam
- dead_fish_seen
- benthic_diversity_notes
- upstream_downstream_context

## safety_notes
- Citizen observations are useful for triage but cannot prove exact pollutant levels.
- Avoid direct contact with unknown water.

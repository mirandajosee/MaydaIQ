# Electrical Hazards Playbook

source_id: local:electrical_hazards

## purpose
Guide conservative response to downed wires, utility damage, submerged outlets, and energized floodwater risk.

## signs
- Downed or low-hanging wire.
- Sparking, buzzing, utility pole damage, water near electrical equipment, or power outage near flooding.
- Vehicle, fence, water, or tree touching a line.

## immediate_actions
- Stay far away from wires and anything touching them.
- Warn others from a distance.
- Treat floodwater near electrical equipment as potentially energized.

## avoid
- Do not touch wires, poles, vehicles, fences, water, or branches that may be energized.
- Do not drive over wires.
- Do not attempt utility shutoff unless trained and authorized.

## when_to_call_emergency_services
- Call local emergency services or the utility emergency line for downed wires, shock risk, fire, trapped people, or energized water.

## calm_mode_guidance
- Keep utility emergency contacts and asset locations ready.
- Train volunteers to maintain a wide exclusion zone and wait for utility crews.

## safety_notes
- Electricity can travel through water and the ground.
- Only trained utility crews should handle electrical infrastructure.

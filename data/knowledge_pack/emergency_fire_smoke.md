# Emergency Fire And Smoke Playbook

source_id: local:fire_smoke

## purpose
Guide safe action for visible fire, smoke, heat, or suspected building fire.

## signs
- Smoke, flames, heat, burning smell, alarms, or blocked exits.
- People possibly inside or unaccounted for.
- Fire near gas, electrical, or structural hazards.

## immediate_actions
- Evacuate away from smoke, heat, and the building.
- Warn nearby people only if doing so does not delay leaving.
- Move upwind and stay low if smoke is present.

## avoid
- Do not re-enter the building.
- Do not open hot doors or move toward smoke.
- Do not attempt firefighting unless trained, equipped, and escape remains clear.

## when_to_call_emergency_services
- Call local emergency services immediately for smoke, flames, alarms, trapped people, injury, or fire near a structure.

## calm_mode_guidance
- Review evacuation routes, assembly points, alarm coverage, and access for responders.
- Keep building information ready: entrances, hydrants, utility shutoffs, and occupancy notes.
- Run evacuation drills without creating panic or blocking exits.

## responder_packet_fields
- visible_smoke_or_flames
- people_unaccounted_for_unknown
- building_type
- safe_access_point
- utility_or_gas_hazards

## safety_notes
- Smoke exposure can become dangerous quickly.
- The safest default is evacuation and official emergency response.
